Outliers by Malcolm Gladwell, Finished 6/10/2018

  • By the Matthew Effect, the rich get richer and the poor stay poor
    • People who start with a small advantage eventually snowballs into having a huge advantage
    • People who are a bit behind don't get opportunities to help them catch up
  • To be good at a certain thing (world professional level), you have to do it for 10 000 hours
  • To become extremely proficient in a skill, you only have to be "good enough", after, it is just hard work*
  • Circumstances can also lead you to success, the environment you are born in, the year/moth you were born in, family background, and culture
  • "Knowledge of a boy's IQ is of little help if you are faced with a formful of clever boys" means specific IQ doesn't matter when they are all "smart enough"
  • Intellect and achievement are far from perfectly correlated
  • Practical intelligence is knowing what to say to whom, knowing when to say it and knowing how to say it for maximum effect
    • It is procedural: knowing how without knowing why and being able to explain it
  • General intelligence and practical intelligence is orthogonal
  • No one person makes it, mentors are important in life
  • Three things make work satisfactory: autonomy, complexity, and a connection between effort and reward

The Wealthy Barber Returns by David Chilton, Finished 01/11/2019

  • Disposable Income = Consumption + Savings
  • The reason it is so difficult to save is because nobody wants you to
  • Some think they need things when they really don't
  • "Some individuals have riches just as we say have a fever when really the fever has us" - Seneca
  • Few things in life are more valuable than our self-esteem, yet we often willingly surrender it to the opinions of others, and we spend a king's ransom trying to get it back as we buy things to try to elevate status
  • "I can resist everything except temptation" - Oscar Wilde
  • We aren't wired to resist temptation, but we can take away the ability to give into temptation
  • The price we are willing to pay is based on our assessment of how much value the item will bring to our lives, but it is much more difficult to assess over a certain period of time
  • Having friends with more money makes us want to "get on their level"
  • "Gratitude is riches. Complaint is poverty" - Doris Day
  • "I can't afford it" is a very powerful statement when declining invitations
  • As we spend money on something nice, we might want other things in our life to match it, spending begets spending
  • "Good debt" is any money borrowed to buy an appreciating asset where the cost of servicing the loan doesn't affect your ability to save to the appropriate level and where the principal will be fully repaid before your retirement
  • Live in a house you can truly afford
  • Pay yourself first: save first, spend the rest
  • Keeping a detailed summary of all your expenses over a period of several months is extremely effective
    • The three areas that catch most people off guard are cars, dining out, and the little things
    • These are the areas where people spent more than they thought
  • If you buy things, make sure those things are splurge-worthy
    • This makes people efficient spenders
  • By cutting back on a few little things, especially habitual purchases, people can save a large amount of money
  • "Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship"
  • "I spent a lot of money on booze, birds, and fast cars. The rest I just squandered" - George Best
  • Prioritize, think before you spend and don't wear the costume of consumer debt to pretend you're wealthier than you are
  • Figuring out how much money needs to be set aside for retirement is difficult for many factors that involve it
    • Age of retirement, death date, tax rates during retirement years, severity of inflation, health-care costs, government plans, rate of return, etc
  • "The best time to plant an oak tree was 10 years ago. The second best time is now" - Roy Miller
  • "Most men do not understand how great a revenue is economy" - Cicero
  • Spend more on experiences and less on stuff
  • "The best things in life aren't things"
  • "Real happiness is cheap enough, yet how dearly we pay for its counterfeit" - Hosea Ballou

12 Rules of Life by Jordan B. Peterson, Finished 3/30/2019

Rule 1: Stand Up Straight With Your Shoulders Back
  • It is a winner's take all situation in the lobster and human world, where 1% of people take 50% of the loot
  • "To those who have everything, more will be given; from those who have nothing, everything will be taken" Matthew 25:29
  • "It's not what we don't know that gets us in trouble. It's what we know for sure that just ain't so" - Mark Twain
  • Like the representation of the ying and yang, chaos and order are eternally juxtaposed
  • Change may be opportunity, instead of disaster
  • You can delay gratification, without forgoing it forever
  • Depression can be a positive feedback loop
    • They feel useless and burdensome
    • This makes them withdraw from contact with friends and family
    • The withdrawl makes them feel lonesome and isolated and more likely to feel useless and burdensome
    • They withdraw more, etc
  • If you say no, early in the cycle of oppression, the scope for the oppression will remain properly bounded and limited
  • No one likes to be pushed around, but people often put up with it for too long
  • When people realize their own capacity for evil, their fear of themselves acting out decreases. They develop self-respect. They begin to resist oppression as they see that they have the ability to withstand as they are terrible too
  • There is very little difference between the capacity for mayhem and destruction and strength of character
  • If you slump, your brain assigns you a low dominance number. Your brain produces less serotonin. This will make you less happy and more likely to back down. You will get lower quality resources. You might abuse substances. It will increase your susceptibility to illness
  • Those who start to have will probably get more
  • If you start to straighten up, people will look at and treat you differently
  • Standing up straight with your shoulders back is not something purely physical; it also affects your psyche
    • You respond to a challenge instead of bracing for a catastrophe
    • You see the gold the dragon hoards instead of fearing the dragon
Rule 2: Treat Yourself Like Someone You Are Responsible For Helping
  • People are better at filling and properly administering perscription medications to their pets than to themselves
  • Chaos is the domain of ignorance; it's unexplored territory
  • Order, by contrast, is explored territory
  • To straddle a fundamental duality, that we eternally inhabit order surrounded by chaos, is to be balanced: to have one foot firmly planted in order and security, and the other in chaos, possibility, growth and adventure
  • The worse of all possible snakes (Garden of Eden reference) is teh eternal human proclivity for evil; it is psychological, spiritual, personal, internal; no walls, however tall, will keep that out
  • It is far better to render Beings in your care competent than to protect them (parenting)
  • If we wish to take care of ourselves properly, we would have to respect ourselves, but we don't, because we are fallen creatures
  • Carl Jung's "doing unto others as you would have them do unto you" or "loving your neighbours as yourself" has nothing to do with being nice, both are equations
  • You do not belong simply to yourself; you can't tortue or mistreat yourself because you are inevitably connected with many others
  • We all deserve some respect, and we must consider what would be truly good for us
  • "Happy" is not synonymous with "good"
  • "He whose life has a why can bear almost any how" - Friedrich Nietzsche
Rule 3: Make Friends With People Who Want The Best For You
  • People choose friends who aren't good for them for a reason
    • Sometimes it's because they want to rescue someone
      • Not everyone at the bottom wishes to rise
      • It is not easy to distinguish between someone truly wanting and needing help and someone who is exploiting and willing helper
    • How do you know that your attempts to pull someone up won't instead bring them, or you, further down
  • It's easier to put off until tomorrow what needs to be done today and drown the upcoming months and years in today's cheap pleasures
  • "It is impossible to convince someone to change for the better" - Carl Rogers
  • Self sacrifice might just be a person trying to look good, pretending to solve what appreas to be a difficult problem instead of actually being good and addressing something real
  • It is not easy to surround yourself with good, healthy people"
    • It requires strength and daring to stand by their side
    • It takes humility, courage, and judgment
Rule 4: Compare Yourself To Who You Were Yesterday, Not To Who Someone Else Is Today
  • No matter how good you are at something, or how you rank your accomplishments, there is someone out there who makes you look incompetent
  • The winners don't take all, but they take most
  • Growing is an important form of winning
  • Be cautious when comparing yourself; you have your own particular set of problems - financial, intimate, psychological, or otherwise
  • Working towards a goal, we are always at point A, moving towards point B
  • To focus, we must pick one thing above all else to focus on
  • You need to know what the problems are because you can't fix something if you don't know it's broken - and you're broken
  • The past is fixed, but the future could be better
  • Happiness should be focused in the journey uphill, and not in the fleeting sense of satisfaction awaiting at the next peak
  • There is an inside voice within you, once burnt and twice shy
  • What you aim at determines what you see
    • Perhaps what you really need is right in front of your eyes, but you cannot see it because of what you are currently aiming for
    • You cannot aim yourself at anything if you are completely undisciplined and untutored
  • Search until you find something that bothers you, that you could fix, that you would fix; that might be enough for the day
  • Attend to the day, but aim at the highest good
  • Realization is dawning. Instead of playing the tyrant pay attention. Tell the truth instead of manipulating the world. Instead of playing the tyrant or martyr, negotiate. You don't have to be envious because you no longer know that someone truly has it better than you. **You don't have to be frustrated if you learn to aim low and be patient** You are discovering who you are, what you want, and what you are willing to do. You are finding specific solutions to your specific problems. You are less concerned at what others do, because you yourself have plenty to do

  • Many adults think that any suggestion that all constraints placed on children are damaging
    • This is lazy, cruel, and inexcusable
  • It's peace that's the mystery, violence is the default

  • Parents must reward those attitudes and actions that will bring their child success in the world outside the family, and use threat and punishment when necessary to eliminate behaviours that will lead to misery and failure
