Book Notes


Outliers by Malcolm Gladwell, Finished 6/10/2018

  • By the Matthew Effect, the rich get richer and the poor stay poor
    • People who start with a small advantage eventually snowballs into having a huge advantage
    • People who are a bit behind don't get opportunities to help them catch up
  • To be good at a certain thing (world professional level), you have to do it for 10 000 hours
  • To become extremely proficient in a skill, you only have to be "good enough", after, it is just hard work*
  • Circumstances can also lead you to success, the environment you are born in, the year/moth you were born in, family background, and culture
  • "Knowledge of a boy's IQ is of little help if you are faced with a formful of clever boys" means specific IQ doesn't matter when they are all "smart enough"
  • Intellect and achievement are far from perfectly correlated
  • Practical intelligence is knowing what to say to whom, knowing when to say it and knowing how to say it for maximum effect
    • It is procedural: knowing how without knowing why and being able to explain it
  • General intelligence and practical intelligence is orthogonal
  • No one person makes it, mentors are important in life
  • Three things make work satisfactory: autonomy, complexity, and a connection between effort and reward

Rich Dad, Poor Dad by Robert T. Kiyosaki with Sharon L. Lechter, Finished 6/23/2018

  • The advice of "study hard and get good grades and you will find a high-paying job with great benefits" may have worked for people born before 1945, but it is disastrous for those of use born in a rapidly changing world
  • Instead of telling children to play it safe, they need to be told to play it smart
    • When a company announces a downsizing, families are hurt and unemployment goes up because people get laid off, but the price of the stock goes up
    • Employees lose, but owners and investors win
  • Life is the best teacher of all, life does not talk to you, it just sort of pushes you around
    • If you learn life's lessons, you will do well and move on, even welcoming the pushing
    • If not, you will continue to be pushed around, some quit while others fight
  • It is easier to change yourself than everyone else
    • Most people want everyone else in the world to change, but themselves
  • The poor and the middle class work for money, the rich have money work for them
  • The "Rat Race" is the pattern of getting up, going to work, paying bills and repeat
    • Give them more money and they just spend more
  • People are controlled by fear and greed, which is intensified by ignorance, so they react instead of think (emotion stands for energy in motion)
    • They keep working thinking money will solve their problems
  • The avoidance of money is just as psychotic as being attached to money
  • If you want to get rich, you need to be financially literate
    • Accounting is very boring, but important for being rich in the long-term
  • Rule 1: know the difference between an asset and a liability
    • Acquire assets, not liabilities (assets put money in your pocket and liabilities take money from your pocket)
  • Money doesn't always solve the problem, it might even speed it up (intelligence solves problems)
  • Professional success is no longer solely linked to academic success
    • People can work harder, but they don't get ahead
  • Financial aptitude is what to do with the money once you make it, how to keep people from taking it away from you, how long you keep it, and how hard that money works for you
  • #1 expense for most people is taxes
  • The reason public speaking are people's number one fear is the fear of standing out, criticism, ridicule, outcast, and being different
  • The Japanese are aware of three powers: the sword, the jewel, and the mirror
    • The sword symbolizes the power of weapons
    • The jewel symbolizes the power of money
    • The mirror symbolizes the power of self-knowledge and is the most treasured
  • An intelligent person hires people smarter than they are
  • When it comes to money, high emotions lower financial intelligence
  • The middle class love to play it safe
    • Mutual funds are popular because they are safe
    • These people would not be able to capitalize on opportunities because of "risk"
  • Wealth is a person's ability to survive so many number of days forward: "If I stopped working today, how long could I survive
  • Mind your own business
    • It is important to keep track of your asset column or whatever makes you money
  • Keep you expenses low, reduce liabilities, and build a base of solid assets
  • Assets fall under into the following:
    • Businesses that don't require your presence
    • Stocks
    • Bonds
    • Mutual funds
    • Income generating real estate
    • Notes (IOUs)
    • Royalties from intellectual property such as music, scripts, patents
  • When investing, once a dollar goes in, don't let it come out, think of it as an employee that works 24/7
  • The reward at the end of the road for diligently taking the time to mind your own business are the luxuries
  • Financial IQ is made up of knowledge from four broad areas of expertise
    • Accounting/Financial Literacy - reading and understanding financial statements, strengths and weaknesses of any business
    • Investing - science of money-making money
    • Understanding Markets - science of supply and demand
    • Law - tax advantage and protection from lawsuits from being aware of accounting, corporate, state, and national rules and regulations
  • Skills are needed to be an investor who creates investments:
    • How to find an opportunity that everyone has missed
    • How to raise money (without a bank)
    • How to organize smart people
  • There is always risk, so learn to manage risk instead of avoiding it
  • Talented people are always one skill away from great wealth
    • When it comes to money, the only skill most people know is to work hard
  • You want to learn a little about a lot (20 hours rule)
  • The hardest part of running a company is managing people
    • If you are not a good leader, you'll get shot in the back
  • It is best to go broke before 30
  • A job is just an acronym for 'Just Over Broke'
  • If baby boomers discover they don't have enough money to live on when they're older, they can blow their brains out with the silver bullet
  • "I'm not interested in going to the gym, but I go because I want to feel better and live longer"
  • The most painful/hardest part is deciding to go/start
  • The management skills needed for success
    • The management of cash flow
    • The management of systems (including yourself and time with family)
    • The management of people
  • The better you are at communicating, negotiating, and handling your fear of rejection, the easier life is
  • The more you give, the more you receive (even if you don't have 'extra' money)
  • 5 reasons why people don't develop proper assets columns
    • Fear - fear of losing money, think Texan: go big or go home, winning usually follows losing, everyone wants to go to heaven, but nobody wants to die, REMEMBER THE ALAMO!!!
    • Cynicism - ignore the Chicken Little, the extra "noise", cynics criticise, winners analyze, criticism blinds while analysis opens eyes
    • Laziness - there is laziness in staying busy, fix laziness with greed, ask "how can I afford it?"
    • Bad Habits - our lives are a reflection of our habits
    • Arrogance - arrogance is just ego + ignorance
  • Texans don't bury their failures
    • They turn them into rallying cries
    • Failure inspires winners and defeats losers
  • To make progress, you must go unbalanced, that's how walking works
  • You must focus to be rich, put a lot of eggs in a few baskets
  • When exiting the rat race, you must ask yourself "How can I afford to never work again?", "What's in it for me if I'm healthy, sexy, good-looking?", "What would my life be like if I never had to work again?", "What would I do if I had all the money I needed"
  • Ten steps/helpful tips
    • You need a reason greater than reality
      • Get a list of emotional "wants" and "don't wants"
      • If you do not have a strong reason, most things will seem like too much work
    • I choose daily
      • Bad thoughts rob you of time and learning
      • Invest first in education
    • Choose friends carefully
      • Learn from all of them, even if it's what not to do
      • When investing, there is always another wave
      • If you miss a wave, search for the next one and get yourself back into position
      • Wise investors buy an investment when it's not popular
    • Master a formula and then learn a new one
      • You become what you study
      • What you know doesn't count anymore because it is probably old
      • It is about how fast you learn
    • Pay yourself first, invest in your assets/mind your business
      • Focus on management skills (see before)
    • Pay your brokers well
      • They are specialized and have great information
    • Be an "Indian Giver"
      • When investing, think about how fast you can get your initial investment back
    • Assets buy luxuries (don't buy on credit)
    • The need for great heroes (mentors/role models) is essential
    • Teach and you shall receive (give what you want first)
  • Some more ideas
    • Stop what you're doing
      • Take a break, step back and analyze
    • Look for new ideas and learn
    • Take action
    • Find someone who has done what you want to do
    • Take classes and buy tapes
    • Make lots of offers

The Wealthy Barber Returns by David Chilton, Finished 01/11/2019

  • Disposable Income = Consumption + Savings
  • The reason it is so difficult to save is because nobody wants you to
  • Some think they need things when they really don't
  • "Some individuals have riches just as we say have a fever when really the fever has us" - Seneca
  • Few things in life are more valuable than our self-esteem, yet we often willingly surrender it to the opinions of others, and we spend a king's ransom trying to get it back as we buy things to try to elevate status
  • "I can resist everything except temptation" - Oscar Wilde
  • We aren't wired to resist temptation, but we can take away the ability to give into temptation
  • The price we are willing to pay is based on our assessment of how much value the item will bring to our lives, but it is much more difficult to assess over a certain period of time
  • Having friends with more money makes us want to "get on their level"
  • "Gratitude is riches. Complaint is poverty" - Doris Day
  • "I can't afford it" is a very powerful statement when declining invitations
  • As we spend money on something nice, we might want other things in our life to match it, spending begets spending
  • "Good debt" is any money borrowed to buy an appreciating asset where the cost of servicing the loan doesn't affect your ability to save to the appropriate level and where the principal will be fully repaid before your retirement
  • Live in a house you can truly afford
  • Pay yourself first: save first, spend the rest
  • Keeping a detailed summary of all your expenses over a period of several months is extremely effective
    • The three areas that catch most people off guard are cars, dining out, and the little things
    • These are the areas where people spent more than they thought
  • If you buy things, make sure those things are splurge-worthy
    • This makes people efficient spenders
  • By cutting back on a few little things, especially habitual purchases, people can save a large amount of money
  • "Beware of little expenses. A small leak will sink a great ship"
  • "I spent a lot of money on booze, birds, and fast cars. The rest I just squandered" - George Best
  • Prioritize, think before you spend and don't wear the costume of consumer debt to pretend you're wealthier than you are
  • Figuring out how much money needs to be set aside for retirement is difficult for many factors that involve it
    • Age of retirement, death date, tax rates during retirement years, severity of inflation, health-care costs, government plans, rate of return, etc
  • "The best time to plant an oak tree was 10 years ago. The second best time is now" - Roy Miller
  • "Most men do not understand how great a revenue is economy" - Cicero
  • Spend more on experiences and less on stuff
  • "The best things in life aren't things"
  • "Real happiness is cheap enough, yet how dearly we pay for its counterfeit" - Hosea Ballou

  • Better to think of struggles as 'To bear this worthily is good fortune'

  • No buts, no excuses

  • "Life leads the willing and drags along the reluctant" - Seneca

  • We may view ourselves as procrastinators or lazy or something else, but we are simply unwilling
  • "Where the willingness is great, the difficulties cannot be great"
  • When we compare ourselves and our own misfortunes to others, we realize that our problems aren't so bad
  • "The best thing you can do is the right thing, the next best thing is the wrong thing, the worst you can do is nothing" - Roosevelt
  • "All I know, is that I know nothing"
  • If you want to win, you have to be willing to be judged by others
  • "You are not defined by what's instead your head. You are what you do"
  • "Great thoughts speak only to the thoughtful mind, but great actions speak to all of mankind" - Roosevelt
  • "Inaction breeds doubt and fear. Action breeds confidence and courage" - Carnegie
  • "I expect nothing and accept everything"
  • If it succeeds, celebrate. if it fails, you can recalibrate
  • Don't expect victory or defeat, plan for victory, learn from defeat
  • Future you is not going to regret a lack of achievement or the absence of any one thing in your life, the only thing you will regret is not trying
  • The seven personal assertions
    • I am willing
      • Either willing to make the change or unwilling to stay the same
    • I am wired to win
      • You accomplish what you say, do, etc, it may be success, but it also may be failure
    • I got this
      • You've went through tough times before and you can do it again
    • I embrace the uncertainty
    • I am not my thoughts; I am what I do
    • I am relentless
      • The best accomplishments are the ones that come from strife
    • I expect nothing and accept everything
